\section{Performance Contract}\label{app:contract}

The tables in this appendix are \emph{benchmark targets}, not measured results. They define the throughput envelopes the deterministic and probabilistic subsystems are expected to meet on the development GPU (\Cref{sec:eval}) and are published as a regression contract in \texttt{docs/BENCHMARKS.md}. They are kept separate from \Cref{sec:eval}, which reports only artifact-backed measurements, precisely so the two are not conflated. Actual throughput depends on graph structure, key distribution, and output cardinality.

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering
  \caption{Deterministic throughput targets.}
  \label{tab:throughput}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Operation} & \textbf{Configuration} & \textbf{Target} \\
    \midrule
    Transitive closure & 100K random edges (sparse)       & ${>}1$M rows/sec \\
    Transitive closure & 1M random edges (medium)         & ${>}5$M rows/sec \\
    Transitive closure & $K_{500,500}$ bipartite (dense)  & ${>}10$M rows/sec \\
    Hash join          & 100K $\times$ 100K               & ${>}50$M rows/sec \\
    Hash join          & 1M $\times$ 100K                 & ${>}100$M rows/sec \\
    Hash join          & High selectivity                 & ${>}20$M rows/sec \\
    Aggregation        & 1M rows, 100K groups             & Tracked; no fixed threshold \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}
